RSI
Relative Strength Index (14-period)
Momentum
Model weight
High
What it is
Measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes on a 0–100 scale. Calculated as the average gain vs. average loss over the last 14 candles.
Why SIGNAL uses it
RSI is the single most reliable momentum indicator for Indian equities. It identifies stocks that are oversold (about to bounce) or overbought (about to correct), with NSE historical backtests showing 68% directional accuracy when RSI crosses key levels.
RSI < 35 → BUY signal (oversold)
RSI > 70 → SELL signal (overbought)
RSI 35–70 → HOLD / neutral

🇺🇸 US markets: Same thresholds work but US stocks tend to stay overbought longer (RSI 70–85) in bull markets. SIGNAL adjusts dynamically.

MACD
Moving Average Convergence Divergence (12,26,9)
Trend
Model weight
High
What it is
The difference between a 12-period EMA and 26-period EMA, smoothed by a 9-period signal line. The histogram shows the strength and direction of momentum shifts.
Why SIGNAL uses it
MACD crossovers (MACD crossing above signal line) are highly reliable on daily NSE charts. Divergence between MACD and price is one of the strongest early-reversal signals the RF model picks up.
MACD crosses above signal → BUY
MACD crosses below signal → SELL
Histogram expanding → trend strengthening
📉
EMA 20 / 50 / 200
Exponential Moving Averages
Trend
Model weight
High
What it is
Exponential moving averages give more weight to recent prices. EMA20 = short-term trend. EMA50 = medium-term. EMA200 = long-term bull/bear market indicator.
Why SIGNAL uses it
EMA crossovers ("golden cross" EMA20 > EMA50) are the backbone of momentum strategies. The RF model uses relative price position vs. all 3 EMAs as features, giving it a multi-timeframe trend picture.
Price > EMA20 > EMA50 → Strong uptrend
EMA20 crosses above EMA50 → Golden cross BUY
Price < EMA200 → Bear territory — caution

Bollinger Bands
BB (20, 2σ) — Upper / Middle / Lower
Volatility
Model weight
Medium
What it is
A 20-period moving average with upper and lower bands at 2 standard deviations. When bands squeeze, a breakout is imminent. When they expand, volatility is high.
Why SIGNAL uses it
BB %B (position within the band) and bandwidth (band width / midline) are powerful features for the RF model. Stocks touching the lower band with high RSI divergence are strong mean-reversion candidates.
Price touches lower band + RSI low → Mean reversion BUY
Bandwidth squeezing → breakout imminent — watch closely
Price above upper band → Overbought — reduce

ADX
Average Directional Index (14-period)
Trend Strength
Model weight
Medium
What it is
Measures the strength of a trend (not direction) on a 0–100 scale. Below 20 = no trend. 20–40 = developing trend. Above 40 = strong trend. Combines +DI and -DI to determine direction.
Why SIGNAL uses it
SIGNAL uses ADX to filter out false signals. A BUY signal from RSI + EMA is far more reliable when ADX > 25, confirming the trend has real momentum behind it rather than being a random sideways fluctuation.
ADX > 25 + +DI > -DI → Confirm BUY
ADX < 20 → choppy market, signals less reliable
ADX > 40 → very strong trend, ride it
📐
ATR
Average True Range (14-period)
Volatility
Model weight
Medium
What it is
Measures average daily price range (high − low) over 14 days. High ATR = high volatility. Used to set dynamic stop losses and targets proportional to the stock's natural movement.
Why SIGNAL uses it
SIGNAL sets stop losses at 1.5× ATR and targets at 3× ATR below/above entry, ensuring a minimum 2:1 risk-reward. This ATR-based position sizing is why SIGNAL's drawdowns stay controlled even in volatile markets.
Stop Loss = Entry − 1.5 × ATR
Target = Entry + 3.0 × ATR
Min Risk/Reward = 2:1
📊
OBV
On Balance Volume
Volume
Model weight
Medium
What it is
Running total of volume: adds volume on up-days, subtracts on down-days. Tracks the flow of money into/out of a stock even when price appears flat.
Why SIGNAL uses it
Volume is the "fuel" behind price moves. When OBV rises while price is flat, institutions are silently accumulating. This divergence is one of the most profitable early signals in SIGNAL's model — especially on NSE midcaps.
OBV rising + Price flat → Accumulation BUY
OBV falling + Price rising → Distribution SELL
OBV confirms price breakout → high-confidence signal

Delivery %
NSE Delivery Volume Percentage
Volume · India-specific
Model weight
High
What it is
Unique to NSE/BSE: the % of total traded volume that results in actual delivery (not squared-off intraday). High delivery = genuine buying conviction. Low delivery = speculative froth.
Why SIGNAL uses it
This is SIGNAL's most India-specific edge. Global algo platforms don't have this data. When delivery % spikes above 70% on a breakout day, it almost always signals institutional buying — one of the highest-conviction BUY signals in the model.
Delivery > 65% + price rising → Institutional BUY ★
Delivery < 25% + price rising → Speculative — caution
Delivery > 80% on breakout → Highest conviction BUY

🇺🇸 US markets: Equivalent data = Dark pool volume + Short interest %. SIGNAL tracks these via FINRA data for US signals.

Stochastic Oscillator
%K (14) and %D (3) — Fast Stoch
Momentum
Model weight
Medium
What it is
Compares closing price to its high-low range over 14 periods. Like RSI but more sensitive to short-term price swings. %K crossing %D gives buy/sell signals.
Why SIGNAL uses it
Stochastic adds a faster, more sensitive momentum layer. When RSI and Stochastic both show oversold simultaneously, the RF model assigns a much higher probability to a bounce — double confirmation.
%K crosses above %D below 20 → Oversold BUY
%K crosses below %D above 80 → Overbought SELL

Volume Surge
Volume vs. 20-day Average Volume Ratio
Volume
Model weight
High
What it is
The ratio of today's volume to the 20-day average volume. A ratio of 2× means today's volume is twice the average — a significant surge worth paying attention to.
Why SIGNAL uses it
Volume surges on breakout days are one of the most reliable breakout-confirmation signals. Stocks with 2× or more volume surges on price breakouts have a 73% continuation rate in SIGNAL's backtests.
Vol ratio > 2× + price breakout → High-confidence BUY
Vol ratio > 3× → major institutional move underway
Vol ratio < 0.5× → low conviction — ignore signal

FII / DII Flow
Foreign & Domestic Institutional Investor Activity
Fundamental · India
Model weight
Medium
What it is
Daily net buying/selling by Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) and Domestic Institutional Investors (DIIs) on NSE. Published daily by NSE/SEBI. FII inflows = bullish macro. FII outflows = risk-off sentiment.
Why SIGNAL uses it
FII activity is the single biggest macro driver of Indian equity markets. When FIIs buy consistently for 3+ days, it creates a tailwind for large-cap signals. SIGNAL uses a rolling 5-day FII net flow as a macro filter.
FII 5d net > +₹3,000Cr → Macro bullish — boost all BUY signals
FII 5d net < -₹3,000Cr → Macro bearish — tighten stops

Twitter / X Sentiment
NLP Sentiment Score — 24h rolling window
Sentiment
Model weight
Medium
What it is
Real-time NLP analysis of tweets mentioning a stock's NSE ticker over the last 24 hours. Outputs a bullish % score, post volume, and 24h sentiment trend (improving/deteriorating).
Why SIGNAL uses it
Retail investor sentiment on Twitter is a leading indicator for small/midcap NSE stocks, often moving 6–12 hours before the price. Used as a secondary confirmation — never a primary signal on its own.
Bullish% > 75% + volume spike → Sentiment confirms BUY
Bullish% < 30% → Negative sentiment — adds to SELL weight
Low tweet volume → sentiment score discounted

🇺🇸 US markets: Reddit WSB sentiment + StockTwits + analyst Twitter accounts are additional sources. SIGNAL assigns higher weight to verified analyst accounts.

Beta & Volatility
52-week Beta vs. NIFTY 50 + Historical Volatility
Risk / Fundamental
Model weight
Lower
What it is
Beta measures a stock's sensitivity to NIFTY 50 moves. Beta 1.5 = 50% more volatile than the index. Historical Volatility (HV) = annualised standard deviation of daily returns.
Why SIGNAL uses it
Used primarily for risk calibration and position sizing recommendations. High-beta stocks get tighter stop losses. SIGNAL also uses beta to normalise signals across stocks of different risk profiles.
Beta > 1.5 → stop loss tightened, smaller position size
Beta < 0.8 → defensive stock, good for long-term holds

Earnings Impact
Quarterly Results Calendar + Historical Earnings Reaction
Fundamental
Model weight
Medium
What it is
SIGNAL tracks the NSE earnings calendar and each stock's historical price reaction to quarterly results (beat/miss). Earnings within 7 days dramatically changes signal confidence.
Why SIGNAL uses it
Earnings events are binary risk events. SIGNAL shows an "earnings flag" on every signal when results are due within 7 days, and adjusts confidence accordingly. Stocks that historically beat estimates by 10%+ get a positive earnings bias.
Results < 5 days away → EARNINGS FLAG — hold or reduce
Historical beat rate > 70% → earnings bullish bias added
Post-results gap-up > 5% → momentum signal boost

Sector Momentum
NIFTY Sectoral Index Performance + Sector Rotation
Macro / Sector
Model weight
Medium
What it is
Relative performance of NIFTY sectoral indices (IT, Bank, Auto, Pharma, Metal, FMCG, Realty) over 5, 10, and 20 days. Identifies which sectors are in favour and which are rotating out.
Why SIGNAL uses it
An individual stock BUY signal is 2× more reliable when its sector index is also in an uptrend. Sector momentum acts as a tailwind multiplier in the RF model — stocks swimming with the tide, not against it.
Sector 10d return > +3% → sector tailwind, boost BUY weight
Sector 10d return < -3% → sector headwind, reduce position size
Sector rotation into IT → higher weight for TCS, INFY signals

🇺🇸 US markets: S&P 500 GICS sector ETFs (XLK, XLF, XLV, etc.) used instead. Fed rate sensitivity by sector is an additional modifier.
